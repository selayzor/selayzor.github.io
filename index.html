About

I am a quantitative methodologist with expertise in psychometrics, item response theory, and educational measurement. I completed my Ph.D. at the University of Georgia, where I developed novel frameworks for applying Diagnostic Classification Models and mixture Rasch models to real-world educational data.

My research sits at the intersection of advanced measurement and social equity — particularly exploring how mixed methods can illuminate the lived experiences of Native learners and professionals in STEM fields.

Fluent in multiple statistical platforms and deeply committed to methodological rigor, I translate complex quantitative findings into actionable insights for researchers and educators alike.

Research Focus

Psychometrics

Diagnostic Classification Models

Developing criteria for item flagging, exploring differential item functioning, and applying DCMs to international assessment data (TIMSS).

IRT

Mixture Rasch Models

Investigating latent class characteristics with covariates to understand fraction computation patterns in mathematics education.

Bayesian

Bayesian Estimation

Examining the role of priors in Bayesian estimation under two- and three-parameter logistic models for robust psychometric measurement.

Equity & STEM

Native STEM Portraits

NSF-funded longitudinal mixed-methods study exploring intersectional experiences and persistence of Native learners and professionals in STEM.
